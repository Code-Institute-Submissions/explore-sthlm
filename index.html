About the City

Stockholm is the capital and most populous urban area of Sweden as well as in Scandinavia. 1 million people live in the municipality, approximately 2 million in the urban area, and 2.4 million in the metropolitan area. The city stretches across fourteen islands where Lake Mälaren flows into the Baltic Sea. Outside the city to the east, and along the coast, is the island chain of the Stockholm archipelago. The area has been settled since the Stone Age, in the 6th millennium BC, and was founded as a city in 1252 by Swedish statesman Birger Jarl. Stockholm is the cultural, media, political, and economic centre of Sweden. The Stockholm metro, opened in 1950, is well known for the decor of its stations; it has been called the longest art gallery in the world. Stockholm is the seat of the Swedish government and most of its agencies, including the highest courts in the judiciary, and the official residencies of the Swedish monarch and the Prime Minister. Stockholm Palace is the official residence and principal workplace of the Swedish monarch.

Vasastan

Residential Vasastan is known for its parks, in particular Vasaparken, a popular picnic spot, and Observatorielunden park, home to the Stockholm Observatory. Tegnérlunden park has statues of Astrid Lindgren, author of the Pippi Longstocking books, and playwright August Strindberg, whose former home, now the Strindbergsmuseet, is nearby. Cozy Scandinavian restaurants cluster on Odenplan and St. Eriksplan squares.

Norrmalm

Norrmalm is a busy commercial area, with mainstream Swedish fashion and design shops lining Drottninggatan and the surrounding streets. It’s also a cultural hub, with opera and ballet at the Royal Swedish Opera, art exhibitions and modern dance at Kulturhuset Stadsteatern and orchestral performances at the neoclassical Stockholm Concert Hall. Tree-lined Kungsträdgården park has walking paths and several cafes.

Östermalm

Elegant Östermalm is known for its smart bars and restaurants on Stureplan square, and its cultural venues, such as the Swedish History Museum, displaying Viking weapons. Designer boutiques dot the area near Östermalms Saluhall, a food market known for traditional specialties like gravlax and smoked shrimp. Centered on the imposing National Library of Sweden, tranquil Humlegården park hosts plays in summer.

Djurgården

Djurgården is a tranquil island known for the Vasa Museum, featuring a 17th-century warship, and the Skansen open-air museum, which has craftspeople and reconstructed homes depicting Swedish life through the decades. Fans of Swedish pop music browse memorabilia, old records and costumes, at ABBA: The Museum. Ferries dock next to Gröna Lund amusement park, known for its thrill rides and summer concerts.

Gamla Stan

The bustling, compact island of Gamla Stan is the city’s old town, with cobbled streets and colorful 17th- and 18th-century buildings. It’s home to the medieval Storkyrkan cathedral and the Royal Palace, the king’s official residence. Stylish bistros serve New Nordic cuisine, while night spots include old-school pubs and chic cocktail bars. On adjoining Riddarholmen island, Riddarholmen Church hosts summer concerts.

Södermalm

Sprawling Södermalm island has a relaxed, creative vibe, with artsy shops, eclectic cafes and the stylish Fotografiska, a contemporary photo gallery in a former industrial building by the water. Classic Swedish restaurants and hip breakfast spots surround leafy Nytorget Square and dot the bustling Hornstull area. In summer, the small beaches in Tantolunden park are popular for swimming and picnics.

Things To Do

Culture

Stockholm Public Library

is a library building in Stockholm, Sweden, designed by Swedish architect Gunnar Asplund, and one of the city's most notable structures. The name is today used for both the main library itself as well as the municipal library system of Stockholm.

The Stockholm City Hall

is the building of the Municipal Council for the City of Stockholm in Sweden. It stands on the eastern tip of Kungsholmen island, next to Riddarfjärden's northern shore and facing the islands of Riddarholmen and Södermalm. It houses offices and conference rooms as well as ceremonial halls, and the luxury restaurant Stadshuskällaren. It is the venue of the Nobel Prize banquet and is one of Stockholm's major tourist attractions.

Visit their website

Fotografiska

is a centre for contemporary photography in Södermalm, with walking distance to Gamla Stan and Slussen. Here you'll find fotographs and art installations all year round. On the top floor you'll also find their restaurant, perfect to stop for a coffee or even lunch or dinner.

Visit their website

Parks & Recreational

Humlegården

is a major park in the district of Östermalm in Stockholm, Sweden. The park borders on Karlavägen in the north, Sturegatan in the east, Humlegårdsgatan in the south and Engelbrektsgatan in the west. It is the location of the Swedish Royal Library.

Kungliga Djurgården

City park accessible by ferry boat & other means, with an amusement park, picnic spots & more.

Kungsträdgården

is a park in central Stockholm, Sweden. It is colloquially known as Kungsan. The park's central location and its outdoor cafés makes it one of the most popular hangouts and meeting places in Stockholm. It also hosts open-air concerts and events in summer, while offering an ice rink during winters.

Leisure Time

Drottninggatan

located in Norrmalm is a major pedestrian street. It stretches north from the bridge Riksbron at Norrström, in the district of Norrmalm, to Observatorielunden in the district of Vasastaden.

Sturegallerian

An exclusive mall in the middle of Stureplan square, with fashion boutiques, restaurants, cafés and the historic Sturebad spa, which opened in 1885.

Gröna Lund

is located in Arenastaden, Solna, next to Solna Station and Friends Arena, only seven minutes from Stockholm city. The center was opened on November 12, 2015 and offers a unique combination of international and exclusive brands, food experiences and entertainment.

was originally built as a steam bakery for the 1897 Stockholm World Fair and is set in charming parkland beside the Skansen open-air museum. Sit on the terrace or in the older, more characterful part of the building. Typical Swedish dishes include the likes of fish roe and sweet and sour herring.

Cafés

Café Foam

Café Foam has been around for quite some time and is a sure favourite among locals to the area. With its on-trend interior, ambitious café food and great coffee - Café Foam is well worth a visit.

Il Caffe

Take a well-deserved coffee break in the heart of busy Södermalm at one of the local’s favorite “fika” spots. Unpretentious and cozy, Il Caffé offers pastries, simple lunches and fresh bread from their own bakery. Check out their other locations, too!

Bröd & Salt

Bröd & Salt is a local chain of artisan bakeries, with 11 locations all over Stockholm. Everything is fresh, made from sourdough, and lactose-free. Besides fresh bread, buns, and cookies Bröd & Salt also has a selection of fresh salads, sandwiches, and wraps if you’re hungry for lunch!
