About The City

The City

Stockholm is the capital and most populous urban area of Sweden as well as in Scandinavia. 1 million people live in the municipality, approximately 2 million in the urban area, and 2.4 million in the metropolitan area. The city stretches across fourteen islands where Lake Mälaren flows into the Baltic Sea. Outside the city to the east, and along the coast, is the island chain of the Stockholm archipelago. The area has been settled since the Stone Age, in the 6th millennium BC, and was founded as a city in 1252 by Swedish statesman Birger Jarl. Stockholm is the cultural, media, political, and economic centre of Sweden. The city is home to some of Europe's top ranking universities, such as the Stockholm School of Economics, Karolinska Institute and KTH Royal Institute of Technology. It hosts the annual Nobel Prize ceremonies and banquet at the Stockholm Concert Hall and Stockholm City Hall. The Stockholm metro, opened in 1950, is well known for the decor of its stations; it has been called the longest art gallery in the world. Stockholm is the seat of the Swedish government and most of its agencies, including the highest courts in the judiciary, and the official residencies of the Swedish monarch and the Prime Minister. Stockholm Palace is the official residence and principal workplace of the Swedish monarch, while Drottningholm Palace, a World Heritage Site on the outskirts of Stockholm, serves as the Royal Family's private residence.

Vasastan

Residential Vasastan is known for its parks, in particular Vasaparken, a popular picnic spot, and Observatorielunden park, home to the Stockholm Observatory. Tegnérlunden park has statues of Astrid Lindgren, author of the Pippi Longstocking books, and playwright August Strindberg, whose former home, now the Strindbergsmuseet, is nearby. Cozy Scandinavian restaurants cluster on Odenplan and St. Eriksplan squares.

Norrmalm

Norrmalm is a busy commercial area, with mainstream Swedish fashion and design shops lining Drottninggatan and the surrounding streets. It’s also a cultural hub, with opera and ballet at the Royal Swedish Opera, art exhibitions and modern dance at Kulturhuset Stadsteatern and orchestral performances at the neoclassical Stockholm Concert Hall. Tree-lined Kungsträdgården park has walking paths and several cafes.

Östermalm

Elegant Östermalm is known for its smart bars and restaurants on Stureplan square, and its cultural venues, such as the Swedish History Museum, displaying Viking weapons. Designer boutiques dot the area near Östermalms Saluhall, a food market known for traditional specialties like gravlax and smoked shrimp. Centered on the imposing National Library of Sweden, tranquil Humlegården park hosts plays in summer.

Djurgården

Djurgården is a tranquil island known for the Vasa Museum, featuring a 17th-century warship, and the Skansen open-air museum, which has craftspeople and reconstructed homes depicting Swedish life through the decades. Fans of Swedish pop music browse memorabilia, old records and costumes, at ABBA: The Museum. Ferries dock next to Gröna Lund amusement park, known for its thrill rides and summer concerts.

Gamla Stan

The bustling, compact island of Gamla Stan is the city’s old town, with cobbled streets and colorful 17th- and 18th-century buildings. It’s home to the medieval Storkyrkan cathedral and the Royal Palace, the king’s official residence. Stylish bistros serve New Nordic cuisine, while night spots include old-school pubs and chic cocktail bars. On adjoining Riddarholmen island, Riddarholmen Church hosts summer concerts.

Södermalm

Sprawling Södermalm island has a relaxed, creative vibe, with artsy shops, eclectic cafes and the stylish Fotografiska, a contemporary photo gallery in a former industrial building by the water. Classic Swedish restaurants and hip breakfast spots surround leafy Nytorget Square and dot the bustling Hornstull area. In summer, the small beaches in Tantolunden park are popular for swimming and picnics.

Things To Do

Culture

ABBA The Museum

is a Swedish interactive exhibition about the pop band ABBA that opened in Stockholm, Sweden in May 2013. ABBA's collected works are showcased in a contemporary, interactive setting at Djurgården, Stockholm.

Skansen

is the first open-air museum and zoo in Sweden and is located on the island Djurgården in Stockholm, Sweden. It was opened on 11 October 1891 by Artur Hazelius to show the way of life in the different parts of Sweden before the industrial era.

The Nordic Museum

is a museum located on Djurgården, an island in central Stockholm, Sweden, dedicated to the cultural history and ethnography of Sweden from the early modern period to the contemporary period.

Parks & Recreational

Humlegården

is a major park in the district of Östermalm in Stockholm, Sweden. The park borders on Karlavägen in the north, Sturegatan in the east, Humlegårdsgatan in the south and Engelbrektsgatan in the west. It is the location of the Swedish Royal Library.

Kungliga Djurgården

City park accessible by ferry boat & other means, with an amusement park, picnic spots & more.

Hagaparken

Hagaparken, or simply Haga in Solna Municipality just north of Stockholm, Sweden, is a vast and popular nature area, with large parks, woods and gardens.

Shopping

Drottninggatan

located in Norrmalm is a major pedestrian street. It stretches north from the bridge Riksbron at Norrström, in the district of Norrmalm, to Observatorielunden in the district of Vasastaden.

Hansakompagniet

Sophisticated mall with fashion stores, a supermarket, a cafe & casual, contemporary eateries.

Westfield Mall of Scandinavia

is a shopping mall located in Solna in Stockholm, Sweden. It was inaugurated on November 12, 2015, and is the second largest mall in the Nordic countries with 224 stores, many of them with double-height storefronts up to 8 m tall. 50,000 shoppers showed up to the opening of the mall.

Breakfast/Brunch

Sthlm Brunch Club

is a cash-free eatery offering hearty breakfast, brunch & lunch staples from eggs to pancakes. Located in Vasastan.

The Greasy Spoon

A modern take on a classic British institution: The Greasy Spoon. Serving a delicious range of breakfast, brunch & lunch items available all day. Two locations in the city, one in Södermalm och one in Vasastan.

Broms

At broms you can have breakfast, lunch and dinner. Shop for treats, cheeses, cold cuts, bread & more in our deli.

Lunch

Riche

Hip bistro with a Swedish/French menu & a quirky decor of bold artworks & chandeliers. Also open for dinner and late night drinks. Located in Östermalm

Bastard Burgers

Bastard Burgers är en snabbt växande streetfoodkedja. Här serveras prisbelönta burgare och Sveriges största veganska utbud. Kärlek till mat och människor har skapat en stark kultur runt Bastard Burgers, där såväl klimatsmarta initiativ som högljudd hiphop ryms. Inspirationen hämtas från New York och kvaliteten kommer från Norrland.

Supper

Supper is a south-american restaurant located in three different cities around Sweden. The original one up north in Åre, two restaurants in the capital Stockholm and one located in Visby, Gotland. Also open for dinner.

Cafés

Vetekatten

Genteel, 1920s-style cafe for self-serve lunches & pastries, snacks, coffee & afternoon tea. Located in Norrmalm.

KSnickarbacken 7

is situated in a 19th century building, with high ceilings and a Gothic influence. The café houses an art gallery, which allows the guests to be surrounded by art pieces during their meal. Must try items include the coffee, and ginger juice, which is freshly made. The café also has a variety of toasts, which make good accompaniments to your coffee.

Chokladkoppen

is a petite café beside the Nobel Museum and Royal Palace. This café serves a wide range of sandwiches and Danish pastries, great complements to a cup of Swedish coffee. Another must try item is the hot chocolate, which is smooth and full of cocoa flavors. The simple designed interior is influenced by the Netherlands, and builds up a comfortable ambience for a relaxing afternoon.

Dinner

Berns Asiatiska

offers flavors from all corners of Asia, primarily Japan, Korea and Vietnam and promises a taste experience beyond the ordinary. The generous menu offers dishes such as Oysters, Gyoza, Korean Steak Tartar, Vietnamese Crêpes and Grilled Seabass Macaostyle. Located in Norrmalm.

Den Gyldene Freden

"Since 1722, Den Gyldene Freden has been a place for people to eat, drink and socialize in a timeless environment. The Nordic ingredients are constantly present, and the food consists of Swedish flavours. Located in Gamla Stan.

Asian Post Office

Innovative Asian dishes, small plates & various cocktails featured in a chic, modern dining room. Located on Östermalm.
